## Abstract
The China Fusion Engineering Test Reactor Central Solenoid Model Coil (CSMC) project, which commenced construction in 2014, is currently in the experimental phase. Experimental advanced superconducting tokamak (EAST) is a superconducting tokamak nuclear fusion experimental device located in China, dedicated to researching the controllable technology of nuclear fusion and currently in continuous operation. The quench protection system serves as the primary safeguard for the operation of the magnets and plays a critical role in ensuring the success of operational experiments. A key component of this system is the voltage acquisition detection lines, which continuously monitor the magnet’s operational status. The safe and reliable operation of these detection lines is essential for the precise functioning of the system. However, the high-voltage detection lines are subjected to harsh environmental conditions, including nuclear radiation, high voltages, ultra-low temperatures, and other extreme factors. Over time, these cables are prone to electrical failures, such as disconnections, short circuits, and insulation damage. This article applies broadband impedance testing [frequency domain reflectometry (FDR)], a mature nondestructive testing technology, to accurately and rapidly detect and locate various electrical faults in the quench detection lines of CSMC and EAST. Real-world field test results for CSMC and EAST verify that FDR can achieve high-precision fault location for twisted-pair and four-core cables, and effectively distinguish typical faults, such as short circuits, open circuits, and water ingress. The application of FDR provides a reliable technical means for the maintenance and safe operation of quench detection systems in superconducting magnet devices, and can prevent latent defects from evolving into permanent faults.
