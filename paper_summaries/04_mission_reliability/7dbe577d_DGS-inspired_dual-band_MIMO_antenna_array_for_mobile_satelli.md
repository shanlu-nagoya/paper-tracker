## Abstract

 This research proposes the design of an efficient and compact multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) antenna with good isolation properties, operating at dual-band resonance frequencies. The dual-band MIMO is intended to cover the frequency bands from 2 to 2.2 GHz and from 2.4 to 2.5 GHz. The first operating band is called the unified S-band (USB), used for mobile satellite applications, and the second band is the most well-known industrial, scientific, and medical (ISM) band, used for wireless applications. The antenna aims to resonate at 2.2 and 2.4 GHz for USB and ISM frequency bands. For this purpose, a compact microstrip patch antenna with a partial ground monopole design and decent radiation characteristics is first proposed. Then, the same design is replicated to form the MIMO. MIMO antennas are beneficial for enhancing reliability, channel capacity, and frequency reuse in modern communication systems. However, it also increases mutual coupling among adjacent electromagnetic radiators and deteriorates the compactness and radiation properties of the overall structure. To reduce coupling, a novel defected ground-inspired decoupling structure is imprinted on the partial ground plane. The unique decoupling structure aims to suppress the coupling effects by disrupting the surface current in the ground plane. It also makes the overall structure more compact by reducing weight and size and enhancing gain and other diversity metrics. The axial ratio within 3 dB, correlation coefficient below 0.05, and diversity gain near 10 are also targeted. The return loss below 25 dB and the insertion loss below 30 dB are maintained in both operating bands, suitable for the prescribed satellite and wireless applications. The antenna is simulated in CST Studio Suite and fabricated on an FR4 substrate with a dimension of 40x48mm2. The simulated results are in good agreement with the experimental results. The comparison with other state-of-the-art MIMO antennas is presented.
