## Abstract
Ultra-reliable low-latency communication (URLLC) has become an integral part of modern industrial wireless networks (IWNs) with rapidly growing number of interconnected sensors. Owing to its notable benefits, nonorthogonal multiple access (NOMA) has become a great choice for IWNs. To enhance resource utilization, user grouping in NOMA systems has been investigated in recent years, focusing on power allocation and sum-rate optimization. This article goes beyond state-of-the-art and proposes a novel user grouping and scheduling algorithm for a hybrid NOMA–OFDMA system tailored to URLLC in IWNs by considering the effective throughput of individual users as a function of bit error rate. First, we formulate an optimization problem to maximize the sum rate while ensuring that the channel gains of the grouped users are sufficiently different and users with similar throughput requirements are grouped together. We propose novel usage of one-stage and two-stage genetic algorithm to find the optimal solution to the user grouping problem in uplink IWN. Scheduling users to physical resource blocks is solved as a bin packing problem. The proposed algorithm is rigorously evaluated based on effective throughput and user fairness. Our findings conclusively demonstrate that the framework not only satisfies the individual throughput demands of each user but also significantly reduces latency compared to the existing order-based grouping and OFDMA. In addition, a notable outcome of the proposed approach is a marked improvement in user fairness, ensuring a more balanced and equitable distribution of network resources among users, which is a critical aspect of enhancing overall network performance.
