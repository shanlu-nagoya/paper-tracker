## Abstract
Although current goal-oriented navigation methods have achieved great performance on object, instance-image, and text-based tasks, they often rely on separate pipelines or heavy training, struggling with generalization and high computational overhead. In this letter, we propose U-KGNav, a unified zero-shot goal-oriented navigation framework built upon a Three-Layer Knowledge Graph: a semantic layer for grounding fine-grained attributes, a spatial layer for efficient frontier-based exploration with trajectory masking, and a rule layer employing a two-stage vision-language reasoning process. Extensive experiments on MP3D and HM3D datasets demonstrate that U-KGNav achieves state-of-the-art zero-shot performance across all three navigation tasks. Real-world deployments on a quadruped robot across four indoor scenarios, including cross-room, long-distance, and dynamic-obstacle cases, further demonstrate its practical feasibility.
