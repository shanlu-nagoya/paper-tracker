## Abstract
Sustainability communication has become increasingly important following the adoption of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which emphasize the need for public awareness and societal engagement in addressing environmental, economic, and social challenges. Mass media plays a significant role in shaping public discourse on sustainability by influencing how these issues are interpreted and prioritized. This study examines how sustainability issues related to the SDGs are framed in Malaysian online mainstream newspapers. Using qualitative content analysis, the study analyses 122 news articles published between 2019 and 2024 across major Malaysian news portals, including The Star, New Straits Times, The Sun, Malay Mail, Free Malaysia Today, The Edge Malaysia, and Bernama. The analysis applies the collective action framing framework developed by Snow and Benford, which identifies three key framing structures: diagnostic, prognostic, and motivational frames. The findings show that prognostic framing dominates sustainability reporting, emphasizing policy initiatives, development programs, and institutional responses to sustainability challenges. Diagnostic framing appears moderately, highlighting environmental and socio-economic problems, while motivational framing is least prevalent, indicating limited emphasis on public mobilization. These findings suggest that sustainability discourse in Malaysian news media is largely policy-oriented and institutionally framed. The study contributes to understanding how media narratives shape sustainability communication and influence public engagement with sustainable development goals.
