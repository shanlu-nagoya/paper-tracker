## Abstract
As core equipment in smart textile production lines, looms require a multidimensional perception of their operational status to establish efficient and reliable in-plant semantic communication (SC). To address strong noise, multivariable coupling, and complex spatiotemporal dependencies in loom sensor data, this article proposes a multidimensional feature extraction method integrating wavelet-enhanced temporal networks and a graph attention mechanism with adaptive edge weighting. A waveletaugmented TimesNet module extracts deep temporal features, while a static prior graph incorporating mechanical knowledge is constructed, and a temporal graph attention network (TGAT) is employed to adaptively learn edge weights that capture varying intersensor dependencies, producing a compact semantic feature vector representing the overall loom status. Experiments on a real industrial dataset show our method achieves 93.7% classification accuracy and maintains robust performance under strong noise (from -10 to 25 dB). It outperforms several mainstream temporal and graph-based baselines and provides a compact, robust, and discriminative feature representation that can serve as a semantic-aware front-end for downstream encoding and transmission tasks in loom-oriented SC systems.
