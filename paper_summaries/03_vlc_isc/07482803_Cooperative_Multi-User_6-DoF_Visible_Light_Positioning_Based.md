## Abstract
In this letter, we propose a camera-photodiode fusion-based 6-degree-of-freedom (DoF) state estimation (i.e., 3D location and 3D pose angles) for cooperative multi-User visible light positioning (CPF-VLP). In the considered model, the camera extracts geometric features of a circular luminaire for 6-DoF state estimation. Subsequently, the orientations of PD–LED modules can be derived from the estimated camera pose, thereby eliminating the need for inertial inputs that are prone to cumulative drift. In addition, PD-LED modules measure the distances between cooperative VLC units using received signal strength (RSS) to refine the estimates and mitigate motion-induced distortions. Simulation results demonstrate that the CPF-VLP algorithm achieves an average positioning error of 1.47 cm.
