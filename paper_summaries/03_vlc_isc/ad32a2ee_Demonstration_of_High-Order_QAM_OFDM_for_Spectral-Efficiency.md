## Abstract
Orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) -based visible light communication (VLC) has emerged as a promising option for 6G scenarios due to its ability to exploit unlicensed optical bandwidth while enabling high spectral efficiency (SE) and flexible allocation of subcarriers (SCs). Nevertheless, the transmission performance of such systems is often restricted by nonlinear distortions originating from optoelectronic components, which constrain the achievable quadrature amplitude modulation (QAM) order. In this work, we establish an SE-enhanced red light-emitting diode-based OFDM-VLC transmission system with high-level QAM orders enabled by employing a high-resolution digital-to-analog converter (DAC) and a linear wideband power amplifier (PA). To further reduce PA-induced nonlinear distortion, discrete Fourier transform (DFT) precoding is applied to lower the signal peak-to-average power ratio (PAPR). Large-size FFT in combination with the intra-symbol frequency averaging (ISFA) technique is utilized to provide accurate channel estimation. Experimental results demonstrate that, with the optimized DAC output current and ISFA taps, up to a 512-QAM DFT-precoded OFDM signal with a record single-wavelength SE of 8.1 b/s/Hz can be successfully transmitted over 1.2-m free-space transmission with the bit error rate below 20% soft-decision forward error correction threshold of $2.4\times 10 ^{-2}$ .
