## Abstract
High-precision on-orbit pointing calibration of the Optical Telescope Assembly (OTA) of laser communication terminal (LCT) is a critical prerequisite for rapid acquisition and tracking of laser inter-satellite links (L-ISL). Current star tracker (ST)-assisted pointing calibration techniques rely on high-precision alignment between visible-light ST and OTA. However, the wavelength mismatch between the ST and the infrared camera of the OTA makes it difficult to calibrate their alignment parameters on-orbit. Furthermore, the inevitable periodic noise introduced into on-orbit telemetry data by space thermal effects and control systems further complicates the determination of high-precision ST-OTA alignment parameters.Therefore, we propose a high-precision on-orbit calibration method for ST-OTA alignment parameters leveraging on-orbit link establishment data to determine them, with Fourier series filtering employed to decouple error components in the frequency domain. Experimental results demonstrate that this method enables on-orbit calibration of ST-OTA alignment parameters with a calculating error less than $40\mu $ rad, meeting the high-precision requirements for L-ISL applications.
