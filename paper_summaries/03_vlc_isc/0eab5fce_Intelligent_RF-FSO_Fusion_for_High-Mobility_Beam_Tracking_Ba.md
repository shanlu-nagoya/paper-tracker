## Abstract
This letter proposes an intelligent strategy for hybrid radio frequency (RF) and free-space optical (FSO) systems, designed to provide robust beam tracking for high-mobility targets with dynamic maneuver. We introduce a novel algorithm in which a reservoir computing (RC)-enhanced unscented Kalman filter (UKF) serves as a cognitive controller. The RC-UKF algorithm learns to adaptively adjust the filter noise covariance matrices and intelligently orchestrate the handover between wide-beam RF acquisition and narrow-beam FSO precision tracking. By leveraging a high-fidelity jerk kinematic model, our system accurately predicts target dynamics. Simulations demonstrate that our approach significantly outperforms the classic KF and standard UKF in tracking accuracy and cost-effectiveness, thereby providing an effective framework for high-capacity RF-FSO hybrid communication in high-mobility scenarios.
