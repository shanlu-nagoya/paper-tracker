## Abstract
Free-space optical (FSO) communication has emerged as a promising technology for high-capacity wireless links, offering advantages such as unlicensed spectrum and immunity to electromagnetic interference. However, atmospheric turbulence introduces intensity scintillation and phase distortions that significantly degrade link performance. To address these impairments, we propose a hybrid probabilistic shaping (PS) and residual carrier modulation (RCM) scheme. A residual carrier is generated by slightly adjusting the bias point of the in-phase and quadrature (IQ) modulator, enabling robust phase recovery without additional pilot tones or high-power direct current (DC) components. Concurrently, PS reshapes the symbol probability distribution to mitigate turbulence-induced intensity fluctuations. Numerical and experimental results show that the proposed PS-RCM approach effectively suppresses turbulence-induced impairments. The proposed method provides a power-efficient and hardware-compatible solution for reliable coherent detection in turbulent FSO channels.
