## Abstract
This letter addresses the achievable rate limitation in multi-user visible light communication (VLC) systems imposed by the low-pass channels of light-emitting diodes (LEDs). We propose a cooperative cell-free multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA) framework and derive a closed-form solution for optimal three-dimensional resource allocation in spatial–frequency–user domain. A key finding is the established equivalence between user and spatial dimensions when global channel state information (CSI) is available. Simulation results show that our NOMA-based resource allocation scheme achieves a significant sum-rate gain over conventional OFDMA by effectively adapting to the band-limited channel. This advantage is more pronounced under heterogeneous user conditions, demonstrating its efficacy for future high-capacity VLC systems.
