## Abstract
In this letter, we examine the physical layer security of a dual-function reconfigurable intelligent surface (DF-RIS)–empowered ambient backscatter communication (AmBC) network, where the DF-RIS simultaneously operates as a reflector and a backscatter device to serve primary users (PUs) and backscatter readers (BRs). The reflected signals are leveraged as cooperative interference to mask the information-bearing backscatter, thereby enhancing communication security. We aim to guarantee secure transmission and maximize the minimum secrecy rate subject to PUs’ quality-of-service (QoS). To tackle the non-convex problem, an alternating hybrid beamforming (AHB) algorithm is proposed, which integrates majorization–minimization (MM) and semidefinite relaxation (SDR) to jointly optimize active and passive beamforming. Simulation results verify the feasibility and effectiveness of the proposed DF-RIS–based secure AmBC framework.
