## Abstract
This article introduces a novel framework for optimizing unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV)-assisted wireless networks to address the dual requirements of data and energy coverage in Internet of Things (IoT) systems. Given the impracticality of replacing or recharging batteries in large-scale IoT deployments, we explore energy harvesting (EH) via UAV-mounted lasers as a sustainable solution. Each IoT device is equipped with an optical receiver with a defined field of view (FoV) to receive both data and energy from UAVs. Unlike conventional radio frequency (RF)-based UAV-IoT systems, laser-assisted links are highly directional and sensitive to geometric alignment, FoV constraints, and atmospheric turbulence, making joint data–energy coverage analysis and optimization particularly challenging. Moreover, existing UAV-IoT or laser communication studies typically rely on planar geometries or point-to-point optical links and do not provide tractable analytical models with joint coverage optimization. In contrast to planar models that ignore the curved geometry of large-scale deployments, our approach places both UAVs and IoT devices on spherical surfaces, naturally defining the FoV through surface orientation and enabling geometrically consistent modeling of directional optical links. We then formulate and solve a complex, nonconvex optimization problem designed to maximize the data coverage probability while ensuring a minimum energy coverage probability by optimizing the UAVs’ altitude and spatial availability. To tackle this, we propose an intelligent solution based on deep reinforcement learning, first developing a tailored deep Q-learning (DQL) baseline and then extending it into a double DQL (DDQL) variant for more robust and unbiased optimization. Monte Carlo simulations validate the model and demonstrate reliable and sustainable data and energy coverage for next-generation UAV-based IoT systems.
