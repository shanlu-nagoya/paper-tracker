## Abstract
This paper presents an optical wireless communication (OWC) transmitter featuring a dual-beam steering system that integrates silicon micro-lenses with piezoelectric actuators. Advancing beyond component-level demonstrations, we achieve full system integration with coordinated dual-beam control and extensive performance evaluation in a realistic deployment scenarios. The system supports wide-angle steering of ±30° in both horizontal and vertical directions, maintaining error-free 10 Gbps data transmission over distances exceeding 3 m. Each beam operates at 9 dBm, below the eye safety threshold. Silicon micro-lenses with a 102.6 μm radius of curvature and a refractive index of 3.48 yields an effective focal length of 41.4 μm and a beam divergence of 4.6°, enabling precise steering. We demonstrate independent parallel operation of two beam steering units, marking an important step toward scalable multi-beam OWC systems for high-density indoor environments. The approach offers key advantages including low interference, enhanced privacy, and compatibility with C-band fiber infrastructure. While beam deformation at large angles and minor actuator hysteresis remain as limitations, the overall performance underscores the system's promise for next-generation indoor wireless communications. ©2026 The Author(s)
