## Abstract
To improve the utilization of spectrum resources and the capacity of anti-eavesdropping is the target of new generation wireless communications. A joint in-band full-duplex (IBFD) and physical layer security (PLS) communication scheme is proposed and demonstrated experimentally, where the self-interference cancellation (SIC) and cooperative-jamming cancellation (CJC) are realized simultaneously by using photonics method. For the SIC and CJC, the phase inversion is obtained by biasing the Mach–Zehnder modulators (MZMs) at the negative quadrature points, while the wide band amplitude and time delay are adjusted in optical domain. The performance of photonic-enabled system with RF propagation via the cable and the air are both carried out at the central frequency of 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz with the bandwidth of 50 MHz and 100 MHz, respectively. For the over-the-air communication, the SIC depth of 17.5 dB and CJC depth of 25.6 dB over 100 MHz bandwidth at the center frequency of 2.4 GHz are realized, and the signal of interest is recovered successfully in the joint IBFD and PLS communication scenario.
