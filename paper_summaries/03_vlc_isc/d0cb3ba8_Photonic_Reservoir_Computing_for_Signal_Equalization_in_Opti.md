## Abstract
Optical wireless communication (OWC) can deliver significantly higher data rates compared to existing radio frequency (RF) wireless systems, reaching the Gbps range. However, at such high data rates, inter-symbol interference (ISI) often occurs due to various factors, such as the limited bandwidth of transmitters and receivers, the multipath effect. The use of conventional electrical digital signal processing (DSP) equalizer for ISI mitigation not only requires expensive high-bandwidth processors but is also power-hungry. In this letter, we explore a new approach for optical-domain signal equalization using photonic reservoir computing (RC). In particular, we investigate the performance of a fabricated RC chip based on a compact microcavity structure with a stadium-shaped design. We first demonstrate temporal diversity among the output signals from the RC chip, which is critical for signal equalization. The performance of the chip is then evaluated at various data rates. Experimental results show that, at data rates up to 5 Gbps, the received signal can be successfully equalized, reducing the bit error rate (BER) from $1.8\times 10^{-2}$ to zero using six output nodes from the chip. Furthermore, by introducing virtual nodes, the chip supports equalization at 10 Gbps, highlighting its scalability. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work demonstrating the application of photonic RC in OWC systems, presenting a novel pathway toward ultrafast and energy-efficient signal equalization in OWC systems.
