## Abstract
In this paper, we present a new transmitter that consists of multibeam generating optical phased array (OPA) and a monolithically integrated electro-optic (EO) modulator, achieving compact system area and high energy efficiency, for optical wireless communication (OWC). A concept of using both path length difference between the routing waveguides in the OPA and wavelength-division multiplexing is demonstrated to simultaneously create multiple beams in free-space and transmit data to receivers at different location, with a small device footprint. To further reduce the power consumption and area of the transmitter, the EO modulator and CMOS driver are co-designed and integrated in a single chip, using 45-nm monolithic silicon photonics technology. The EO modulator leverages an optical digital-to-analog converter structure, supporting both two-level and four-level pulsed amplitude modulations. The chip size of the 101-element OPA is 180 mm2, and an active area of the co-integrated modulator and CMOS driver is 3.4 mm2. The combined OPA and modulator system achieves maximum data rate and power consumption per beam/channel of 54 Gbps and 7.11 pJ/bit, respectively.
