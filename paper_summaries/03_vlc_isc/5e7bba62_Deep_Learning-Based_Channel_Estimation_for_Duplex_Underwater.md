## Abstract
Underwater wireless optical communication (UWOC) offers high-bandwidth underwater connectivity, where its full-duplex form further increases the throughput by bidirectional transmission. However, backscatter interference from the strong transmitted beam severely degrades communication quality. To address this problem, we apply and validate a deep learning channel estimation scheme based on a cascaded super-resolution convolutional neural network and denoising convolutional neural network architecture based on a full-duplex UWOC experimental platform. Using two-dimensional interpolation of sparse pilot least squares estimates as input, the network refines the frequency-domain channel response through super-resolution reconstruction and residual denoising, which suppresses backscatter interference effectively. Experimental results show that the proposed method significantly improves channel estimation accuracy and reduces the bit error ratio.
